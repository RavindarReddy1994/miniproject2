  • A survey says “INDIANS waste as much food as the whole of United Kingdom consumes”.
  • According to the United Nations Development Programme, up to 40% of the food produced in India is wasted.
  • India ranks 63 among 88 countries in Global Hunger Index.
  • About 20% of what we buy in urban India ends up being thrown away.
  • The actual worth of money per year in India from food wastage comes to a whopping 58,000 crore.
  • According to a survey by “Bhook” (an organization working towards reducing hunger) in 2013, 20 crore Indians sleep hungry at any given night. About 7 million children died in 2012 because of hunger / malnutrition.
  • It is revealed that the quantity of wheat wasted by poor storage is equal to the entire production of wheat of Australia.
  • Food wastage may be a crime for which there is no punishment by law (some countries have even put into action certain norms like ‘what you don’t eat, you pay’, which have yielded results);
  • food wastage may be a sin for which there is probably no punishment in terms of heaven and hell.
